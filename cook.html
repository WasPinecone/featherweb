This morning, I gathered fresh fish from the shallow stream and a selection of insects from the forest floor. Preparing them properly ensures both flavor and safety.

Here is a simple method for cooking a combined fish and insect meal:

  • Ingredients:
    • 5â€“6 small freshwater fish, cleaned
    • A handful of edible insects (beetles, grasshoppers, or crickets)
    • A small amount of herbs or edible moss for seasoning
    • Water, for steaming
  • Instructions:
    • Clean the fish carefully, removing scales and any inedible parts.
    • Rinse the insects, discarding any that appear damaged or unsuitable for consumption.
    • Heat a flat cooking stone over a fire or a controlled heat source.
    • Place the fish and insects on the stone, sprinkle with herbs or moss as desired.
    • Cover with a larger stone and add a small amount of water to create steam.
    • Cook for approximately 10 minutes, or until the fish flesh is firm and the insects are crispy.
    • Serve on a clean leaf or plate, consuming immediately for best texture and flavor.

This meal is both nutritious and efficient for a life spent in the forest. Steaming preserves moisture and flavor, while the combination of protein from insects and fish makes a balanced diet. Future variations may include additional herbs or foraged plant matter for subtle flavor differences.
